About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Texas State University, working under the supervision of Dr. Vangelis Metsis at the Intelligent Multimodal Computing and Sensing (IMICS) Laboratory. My research focuses on developing innovative machine learning methods for time series analysis, with particular emphasis on Transformer-Based architectures and designing novel positional encoding techniques for temporal data.

My work bridges theoretical advances in machine learning with practical applications in smart health, computer vision, and large language models. I am passionate about creating robust, efficient, and interpretable AI systems that address real-world challenges.
