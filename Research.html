Research Overview

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning, time series analysis, and healthcare applications. I am particularly interested in developing novel deep learning architectures that can effectively model temporal dependencies in sequential data. My work combines theoretical innovation with practical applications, aiming to create AI systems that are both powerful and interpretable.

Current Research Projects

Dynamic Positional Encoding for Time Series Transformers

Developing signal-aware positional encoding techniques that dynamically adapt to the characteristics of time series data, enabling transformers to better capture temporal patterns across different scales. This work introduces DyWPE (Dynamic Wavelet Positional Encoding), which uses wavelet decomposition to create context-aware positional embeddings.

Figure: DyWPE architecture showing signal-aware dynamic wavelet positional encoding

Status: Accepted at ICASSP 2026 | Collaborators: Dr. Vangelis Metsis

Temporal Context Modeling with Label Transition and Recurrent Neural Network

Conventional models often analyze time-series windows in isolation. To address this, we introduced a lightweight Bayesian meta-classification approach that models the transition probabilities between labels of adjacent windows (Irani & Metsis, 2024). By considering the predictions of preceding windows, this method refines the current prediction, enhancing accuracy by incorporating temporal context.

Figure: tri-context architecture showing three stage architecture for short-long term temporal context Modeling

Status: Published at FLAIRS 2024 (Irani & Metsis, 2024), Under Submission NuerIPS 2026 | Collaborators: Dr. Vangelis Metsis, Bikram De
