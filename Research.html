Research Overview

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning, time series analysis, and healthcare applications. I am particularly interested in developing novel deep learning architectures that can effectively model temporal dependencies in sequential data. My work combines theoretical innovation with practical applications, aiming to create AI systems that are both powerful and interpretable.

Research Impact

My work has contributed to advancing the field through:

Publications in top-tier conferences and journals (ICASSP, FLAIRS, IEEE COINS, Expert Systems Journal)

Open-source implementations that enable other researchers to build upon my work (available at IMICS Lab GitHub)

Recognition including Best Student Paper Award at IEEE COINS 2022

Collaborations with leading researchers in machine learning and healthcare

Mentorship of undergraduate and graduate students in research projects
