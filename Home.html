About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Texas State University, working under the supervision of Dr. Vangelis Metsis at the Intelligent Multimodal Computing and Sensing (IMICS) Laboratory. My research focuses on developing innovative machine learning methods for time series analysis, with particular emphasis on transformer-based architectures and novel positional encoding techniques for temporal data.

With over five years of experience in machine learning, I specialize in AI-powered applications for smart health, time series analysis, and soft computing algorithms. My work bridges theoretical advances with practical applications in healthcare, computer vision, and natural language processing. I am passionate about creating robust, efficient, and interpretable AI systems that address real-world challenges.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned my M.Sc. in Information Systems from Shahid Beheshti University (2022), where my thesis on hyperparameter tuning using genetic algorithms received the Best Student Paper Award at IEEE COINS 2022. I also hold a B.Sc. in Physics from the University of Tehran (2018), providing me with a strong foundation in computational and analytical methods.
